About MOPR

The Ministry of Panchayati Raj looks after the ongoing process of decentralization and local governance in the 28 States and 8 Union Territories of India, by way of empowering Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs), capacity building and rural infrastructure development.

The Ministry is responsible for advocacy and coordinated action on policy issues relating to Panchayati Raj, capacity building, incentivization based on performance, monitoring and evaluation, research and knowledge management.

The Ministry also looks after the implementation of various schemes and programmes aimed at strengthening the Panchayati Raj system in the country including the Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan (RGSA), SVAMITVA scheme, and various other initiatives for rural development and governance.

Key Functions

  • Policy formulation and advocacy for Panchayati Raj institutions
  • Capacity building and training of elected representatives
  • Implementation of flagship schemes like RGSA and SVAMITVA
  • Monitoring and evaluation of rural governance initiatives
  • Coordination with State Governments on decentralization
  • Research and knowledge management in rural governance
